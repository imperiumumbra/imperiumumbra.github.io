  • A Neutrostatement

    2/28/2024

    ·

    Justice

    Sun shall dawn, on worlds now shorn

    Freedom stripped to mourn n’ scorn.

    Jan's place hung from lofty airs,

    Nothing found with unturned hairs.

    Space no longer has sublime,

    Empty void remains a crime.

    Never hung from lively strand,

    Lack of desert sun command.

    None shall see the bird, and fruit,

    All gaze on dismayed, acute.


    Monkey-Panther burning bright,

    On that cord of heaven’s light

    What immortal hand or eye,

    Could thy face neutrosofye?


    From what lost, forgot enchant,

    Hails the penguin or eggplant?

    On what wings dare he aspire?

    What the hand, dare seize the fire?


    And what cherry, & what bird,

    Could hang from stem with joyous herd?

    And when the sheep began to bleat,

    With cauliflower, what great feat?


    What the Rabbit? what the Cat,

    In what closet are thy stashed?

    In what dusty, ancient land,

    do Kangaroo and Lion stand?


    When Smarandache loosed his herd

    And Paradoxist art had stirred:

    Did he smile his work to see?

    Did he who make Neutrosophy make thee?


    Monkey-Panther burning bright,

    In the Jan’s Place in the light:

    What a Council or a Clown,

    Dare take thy noble visage down?
